Customer churn prediction is a critical task for businesses, particularly in competitive industries like telecommunications, where customer retention directly impacts revenue and growth. Customer churn refers to the rate at which customers stop using a company’s products or services within a specified period. Understanding why customers are leaving, and predicting churn rates, enables companies to proactively address issues, improve customer satisfaction, and enhance customer loyalty. This project focuses on building a deep learning model to predict customer churn specifically within the telecom sector. The model will analyze key factors contributing to customer churn, such as service quality, customer support interactions, billing issues, usage patterns, and more. By accurately predicting churn, telecom companies can identify at-risk customers and take targeted actions to retain them
